Car-Buying Platform


In early 2017, I met an LA-based corporate attorney and Marine Corps veteran at a gathering for SoCal entreprenuers. We teamed up with a sales and development executive to launch a tech startup in the automotive space which allowed consumers to buy, lease, and finance vehicles 100% online.

Our small company was able to raise hundreds of thousands of dollars in funding from angel investors. We even applied for the Y-combinator. Most of the money we raised went to an NYC-based product-engineering firm which helped us build our platform and to a video productions company which helped us film an online commercial.

I was the company's chief technology officer and I was tasked with managing a contracted team of developers from the product engineering firm we hired. Many of the firm's developers were based overseas, so I worked at the crack of dawn to accomodate their schedules.

With regards to our web application, I was tasked with making it responsive, implementing UI wireframes, and implementing a DataOne CORS plugin.

Unfortunately, our startup venture ended up not working out and we ended up losing a lot of time and money in the process, but it was a valuable learning experience and it provided me with the opportunity to develop my leadership and management skills.
