About Me

Hi, I'm Marisol Jimena Figueroa. I'm a 30-year-old Mexican-American female. I was born in New Rochelle, New York, about 15 minutes from The Bronx. And I grew up in San Diego, California, about 10 minutes from the U.S./Mexico border.

I am of Mexican descent and I was raised in the Mexican culture. My parents are from Mexicali, Baja California and Piedras Negras, Coahuila. They immigrated from Mexico to the U.S. as adults. Spanish is my native language.

My hobbies and interests include DJing, music production, hiking, yoga, boxing, muay thai, drumming, playing the Bb clarinet, and watching Spanish-language telenovelas.

I’ve had a lifelong interest in technology and coding. I started programming computers in high school. And in my late teens, I became involved in coding meetups, tech startups, hackathons, and programming competitions.

In 2015, I got my first software engineering internship at AudaExplore, a Solera Company. I performed so well in the role that immediately after the internship ended, I was offered a position there as a software engineering contractor. I was the only intern among the summer 2015 batch that was asked to stay. During my time at AudaExplore, I worked on the mobile team, developing web and mobile apps for car insurance companies. I also had the pleasure of organizing a hackathon, performing quality assurance testing, and engaging in automation engineering. However, unfortunately, in 2016, the company decided to move its corporate headquarters to Utah and let go of all its San Diego employees.

Afterwards, I got a job as a software developer at a Chicago-based financial firm called Milesius Capital Resources. I had always been interested in finance, and during my time there, I became immersed in the world of high-frequency options trading.

When my contract there ended, I decided to shift directions and go into business for myself. I launched my own software consulting business. I would get involved with professional societies and create online profiles to generate client leads. I soon found myself juggling several projects at a time. My clients ranged from early-stage startups, sole proprietors, and mid-stage startups.

Eventually, I co-founded my own tech startup in the automotive space with a corporate attorney I met at a tech meetup. As the company's chief technology officer, I was tasked with managing a team of developers at an NYC-based product engineering firm. Because many of the firm's engineers were based overseas, I found myself working at the crack of dawn to accommodate their schedules. Unfortunately, despite landing several investors, our startup ended up not working out. Still, I am grateful for the experience and feel that I was able to develop my management skills and grow as a leader.

Ever since I was a teenager, I have been DJing as a hobby. I have had a lifelong passion for music and in my 20s, I enjoyed exploring the nightlife scene. I began picking up paid DJ gigs at local bars and nightclubs. Eventually, I was landing so many paid gigs that I realized I could turn this passion into a career. In 2017, I decided to launch my own DJ company. It evolved into a successful audiovisual productions company called Awesome Audiovisual Productions, which I'm still running to this day. I have held DJ residencies at bars, nightclubs, entertainment venues, sporting events, music festivals, retail stores, parades, and restaurants all over Southern California. I have had several high-profile clients including but not limited to California Lieutenant Governor Eleni Kounalakis, the California Democratic Party, and the U.S. Navy.

I attended the Global BPM DJ Academy to learn DJing and music mixing and mastering techniques. I was taught there by Antonio Aguilera (AKA DJ Yodah), who became my mentor. I later began taking music production classes at San Diego City College for fun. I ended up taking so many music classes there that I figured I might as well pursue an associate's degree in digital music technology. During my time at City College, I became an officer for the Music Club. I was put in charge of the Music Club's podcast and also became involved with the campus recording studio, MIDI Lab, and radio station. I graduated from San Diego City College in May 2021 with a 4.0 GPA in my major and a 3.97 overall.

Immediately after graduating from San Diego City College, I transferred to Northern Arizona University. My education at NAU was 100% free because I received a generous grant from the school that covered all my expenses. I majored in liberal arts, so I took courses in literature, history, philosophy, political science, art, and sociology. My courses involved lots of reading and writing. I enjoyed them because I've always been an avid reader and fascinated by the humanities. If I didn't have to worry about making money, I'd spend a good portion of the day reading classic literature.

I am set to completed my bachelor's degree at Northern Arizona University in May 2023. I am seeking out full-time job opportunities and I am very excited to grow and apply my skills and experience toward my new role.

If there's anything else you'd like to know about me, feel free to ask. I'm an open book.
